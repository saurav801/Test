Critical Deviation Identified: ULS MGO Under-Reporting in DPN
The DPN daily noon report records 486.6 mt less ULS MGO than the MRV flow-meter system — a +19.0% discrepancy. All other fuel types (HS HFO, VLS HFO, VLS LFO) match to within rounding tolerance (<0.1 mt). The deviation is concentrated in the at-sea phase (+601.8 mt in XLSX vs CSV), partially offset by higher in-port ULS MGO in the CSV (+116.2 mt). This suggests systematic under-reporting of auxiliary engine ULS MGO consumption during sea passages in noon reports.

Root Cause Analysis
Why the deviations exist
C1
ULS MGO: Flow Meter vs. Noon Estimate (Primary)
The MRV system uses 100% flow meter measurement (Method C), which provides continuous, automated fuel measurement. DPN noon reports rely on officer-estimated consumption submitted once daily. At-sea AE fuel consumption — particularly ULS MGO for auxiliary engines and boilers during sea passages — is chronically underestimated in manual noon reports. The +601.8 mt at-sea shortfall strongly supports this. The −116.2 mt in-port over-reporting in DPN may reflect officers rounding in-port consumption upward, slightly compensating but not resolving the overall deficit.
C2
Distance: Calculated vs. Reported (Minor)
The DPN calculates distance at 39,370 nm (GPS track-based) vs. the officer-reported distance of 39,524 nm. The MRV system uses 39,520.2 nm — aligned closely with the officer-reported figure (−3.8 nm). The 150.2 nm gap between DPN calculated and MRV distances is expected and not a source of concern; it reflects routing curves and GPS sampling intervals vs. navigation officer estimates.
C3
Time: Full Year vs. Voyage Segments (Scope Difference)
The 3,502-hour gap in time coverage is entirely structural, not a data error. The DPN reports the complete calendar year (8,760 hrs = 365 days × 24 hrs), including all idle and port time. The MRV XLSX sums only declared voyage legs and in-port stays (5,257.65 hrs). The remaining ~3,502 hrs represent idle/waiting periods not classified as part of a leg — consistent with an AFRAMAX tanker's typical operational profile.
C4
HS HFO / VLS HFO / VLS LFO: Rounding Only
All three heavy fuel categories match to within ±0.08 mt across the full year — differences attributable purely to decimal rounding between systems. These fuel types are almost certainly measured by the same flow meters or tank sounding methods reported consistently across both systems, confirming data integrity for HFO categories.
